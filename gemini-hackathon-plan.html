Gemini Tokyo Hackathon

AI Welfare-Check Voice Companion

A Japanese voice agent for elderly people living alone that remembers prior conversations, detects risk in real time, adapts naturally, and alerts caregivers only when evidence warrants it.

Not an IVR Conversation is adaptive, not a fixed questionnaire.
Memory-enabled Prior pain, mood, habits, and context shape each call.
Realtime risk Risk is evaluated during the call, not only afterward.
Evidence-based alerts Caregivers see why an alert was created.

Why This Fits The Hackathon

The project uses Gemini for live voice reasoning and Google Cloud for deployment, state, and a working demo. It is locally relevant to Japan's aging population and easy to explain in a short live presentation.

Product Goal And Differentiator

Goal

Help families and caregivers notice changes in well-being earlier through short, natural, recurring voice conversations.

Differentiator

The agent builds longitudinal understanding. It knows who it is talking to, what was previously mentioned, what risk signals matter now, and when enough information has been gathered.

Traditional IVR / Checklist
  • Same questions every day.
  • No memory of prior concerns.
  • Escalation feels rule-based.
Memory-Enabled Welfare Agent
  • Starts from the person's history and habits.
  • Adapts based on weak signals during the call.
  • Escalates with evidence and context.
Positioning: AI well-being and welfare-check companion. Not an AI doctor, not a diagnosis assistant, and not a replacement for emergency services.

Realtime Adaptive Conversation Flow

The agent is driven by conversation goals, not fixed questions. After each important user turn, the risk evaluator updates known facts, uncertainty, risk level, and the next best conversation goal.

M Memory Context

Loads profile, prior pain, mood trend, habits, and last known concerns.

V Voice Agent

Starts naturally in Japanese and asks one relevant question.

U User Answer

Speech is interpreted for facts, tone, weak signals, and missing context.

N Next Goal

Planner decides whether to reassure, clarify, follow up, or escalate.

R Risk State

Risk score, evidence, uncertainty, and open questions update during the call.

! Alert Surface

Only creates a caregiver alert when the evidence threshold is met.

Known factFell yesterday
Risk signalDizzy when standing
UncertaintyInjury severity
Next goalClarify safety gently
AI 先週、膝が痛いとおっしゃっていましたが、その後どうですか？ Last week you mentioned your knee was hurting. How has it been since then?
Elder 昨日ちょっと転んで、今日は立つとふらつきます。 I fell a little yesterday, and today I feel unsteady when I stand.
AI それは心配ですね。今はお一人ですか？けがや痛みはありますか？ That is concerning. Are you alone right now? Do you have any injury or pain?

Demo Scenarios

Scenario 1

Normal Personalized Check-In

Memory says Sato-san had knee pain last week and sounded tired yesterday.

今日はまあまあです。膝は少し良くなりました。
I am doing okay today. My knee is a little better.

Expected result: no alert, knee memory updated, dashboard shows stable.

Scenario 2

Subtle Loneliness / Mood Decline

User does not report a medical issue, but hints at isolation.

別に大丈夫です。ただ、最近あまり人と話していません。
I am not really in trouble. I just have not talked to people much lately.

Expected result: moderate concern, soft follow-up suggestion, no urgent alert.

Scenario 3

Fall / Dizziness Escalation

The strongest live demo because it shows memory, adaptive questioning, and escalation.

昨日ちょっと転んで、今日は立つとふらつきます。
I fell a little yesterday, and today I feel unsteady when I stand.

Expected result: alert for daughter with evidence: fall, dizziness, alone, pain.
